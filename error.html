Rules8 Grammar Rules You Must Know
⏰
1. Tense Consistency (34%)
In a past-tense narrative, every verb must stay in the past. Switching to present is the #1 error type.
✗ Wrong
"He is imposing his will upon the little black boy."
✓ Correct
"He was imposing his will upon the little black boy."
✗ Wrong
"We doesn't worry because there had been explosions before."
✓ Correct
"We didn't worry because there had been explosions before."
👤
2. Pronoun Agreement (18%)
Reflexive pronouns (himself/herself/itself/themselves) must match the subject. Object pronouns (him/her/it) must match the noun they replace.
✗ Wrong
"His distraught mother couldn't bring himself to hold him."
✓ Correct
"His distraught mother couldn't bring herself to hold him."
✗ Wrong
"I puzzled within himself as I went down the road."
✓ Correct
"I puzzled within myself as I went down the road."
⚖️
3. Subject-Verb Agreement (16%)
A plural subject needs plural verb and plural noun. Singular subject (He/She/It) needs -s/-es on verb. "Both were little boys" not "boy".
✗ Wrong
"They were both very little boy, one was four years old."
✓ Correct
"They were both very little boys, one was four years old."
✗ Wrong
"My parents was truly proud of me."
✓ Correct
"My parents were truly proud of me."
📰
4. Articles a/an/the (10%)
Use an before vowel sounds. Use the for specific/known nouns. Errors like "a young man" vs "an young man" appear frequently.
✗ Wrong
"Socrates told an young man to meet him near the river."
✓ Correct
"Socrates told a young man to meet him near the river."
✗ Wrong
"It's in fire." (preposition error disguised as article)
✓ Correct
"It's on fire."
🔤
5. Word Form / Spelling (9%)
Adjective used where adverb needed (definite→definitely), wrong spelling (explosion→explose), wrong form (managing→management).
✗ Wrong
"He was definite a little Jamaican."
✓ Correct
"He was definitely a little Jamaican."
✗ Wrong
"We heard the explose early in the morning."
✓ Correct
"We heard the explosion early in the morning."
🔗
6. Preposition Errors (7%)
"On fire" not "in fire". "vomited" not "to vomited". Prepositions are tricky — learn fixed phrases.
✗ Wrong
"He left the hospital room to vomited."
✓ Correct
"He left the hospital room to vomit." (infinitive form)
✗ Wrong
"Both were dressed on blue shirts."
✓ Correct
"Both were dressed in blue shirts."
🔢
7. Number Agreement (4%)
Countable nouns must be plural when the context demands it. "much years" → "many years". "These were her last words" not "word".
✗ Wrong
"Nick and his parents spent much years asking why."
✓ Correct
"Nick and his parents spent many years asking why."
✗ Wrong
"She did right everything during pregnancy."
✓ Correct
"She did everything right during pregnancy." (word order)
🔀
8. Conjunction / Word Order (2%)
Wrong conjunction choice or wrong word sequence. "don't" cannot follow "You have" → "You don't have to". Word order matters.
✗ Wrong
"You have don't to spend money on new shoes."
✓ Correct
"You don't have to spend money on new shoes."
✗ Wrong
"Everyone stopped who they were doing."
✓ Correct
"Everyone stopped what they were doing."
StrategyHow to Score 5/5 in the Exam
01
Read the full passage first
Before looking at individual sentences, read the whole passage. This tells you the tense (past/present) and topic — both help spot errors instantly.
02
Check tense first, always
34% of errors are tense. Ask: "Is this passage in past tense?" If yes, every verb should be past. Any present tense verb is likely the error.
03
Check pronouns second
Look for himself/herself/itself. The reflexive must match the subject. "Mother…herself", "Father…himself", "I…myself". 18% of marks here.
04
Look for singular/plural mismatch
"They were both very little boy" → boys. Whenever "they/both/all" appears, check if the noun that follows is plural. Very common pattern.
05
Write full corrected sentence
Don't just write the correct word. Rewrite the COMPLETE sentence. Examiners give marks only for the full corrected sentence. Never skip this.
06
One error per sentence only
Every numbered sentence has exactly ONE error. Once you find it and fix it, don't change anything else in the sentence. Over-correction loses marks.
